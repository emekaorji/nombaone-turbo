## The scenario

You are choosing a subscriptions layer, or deciding whether to keep building your own. Every option demos
beautifully: a plan, a checkout, a green success. The demo is not the thing that will page you at 2am. These
are the questions that will.

## The naive approach

Judge it by the happy path. Watch a charge succeed, admire the dashboard, check that the SDK is pleasant, and
move on. If it can bill a card in the demo, surely it can run your revenue.

## Why it breaks

Billing is defined by its failures, not its successes. A layer that has never been killed mid-run, never met a
thin balance, never reconciled a short transfer, looks identical to one that has, right up until the month it
costs you real money and real trust. The gap between "it charged a card once" and "it will not lose or double
your money under load" is the entire product.

## What to actually check

Ask each of these, and require a mechanism, not a reassurance.

- **Double-charge safety.** If the billing job is killed two-thirds of the way through and restarts, can the
  same period be charged twice? The answer should be a database constraint you can name, not "we are careful."
- **Involuntary churn.** When a charge fails, does the retry know why it failed and when the customer gets
  paid? A fixed retry ladder in a thin-balance market is lost revenue by design.
- **Push rails.** Can it bill a customer who can only pay by bank transfer, and does it confirm the money
  actually landed before marking the invoice paid, or does it trust the notification?
- **The ledger.** Is money integer-valued and the ledger immutable, or are there floats and in-place updates
  waiting to drift a kobo at a time?
- **Webhooks.** Are they signed, retried, and replayable, with delivery stated honestly as at-least-once so
  your side can dedupe? Anyone promising "exactly-once" is promising something the network cannot give them.
- **Isolation.** Is tenant isolation a property of the data model, enforced on every query, or a middleware
  check that one forgotten route can bypass?

## How Nomba One answers, plainly

Double-charge safety is a unique constraint on the subscription and the period, not hopeful code. Dunning is
payday-biased and classifies failures, so a thin balance is retried when money arrives and an expired card is
not retried at all. Transfers verify-then-requery and match the exact amount before settling. The ledger is
integer kobo, immutable, and append-only. Webhooks are signed, at-least-once, deduplicated, retried, and
replayable, never exactly-once. Isolation is `organization_id` and `environment` on every row, enforced from a
trusted key, not a middleware afterthought.

And the honest part: direct-debit mandates, payouts, refunds, and escrow are built but still live-gated behind
the payment provider. We describe them as capabilities we have implemented, not as things we have proven in
production at scale. A billing layer that will not tell you which of its features are live yet is not one to
trust with your revenue.
