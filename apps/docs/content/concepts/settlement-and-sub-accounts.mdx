---
title: Settlement & payouts
description: How a verified collection splits into the platform fee and the merchant's net, accrues to a balance they own, and reaches their bank account.
---

When a subscription charge is collected and verified, the money doesn't just sit in one
pool. It **splits at collection**, and each organization's share becomes a balance that
belongs to them.

## Split at collection

A verified collection is split into two legs, recorded in the [ledger](/concepts/the-ledger):

- **The platform fee**: the fee your platform charges, computed by the fee engine.
- **The organization's net**: what's left, which belongs to the merchant.

The split is posted as balanced ledger legs, so gross always equals fee plus net, to the
exact kobo. A single kobo cannot be invented or lost between them.

## Where a merchant's money lives

An organization's money is a **balance in the ledger** — a liability we owe them, credited
the moment a payment settles. It exists from the day they sign up. There is nothing to
connect, provision, or verify before billing works, and no way for an organization to be
half-onboarded into a state where it collects money it cannot see.

Multi-tenancy is a property of that ledger: one integration can run many merchants, each
with their own customers and their own isolated money. See
[isolation is a data-model property](/concepts/hard-parts/isolation-is-a-data-model-property).

<Callout type="note" title="One number, one place">
  The balance a merchant is shown is the same ledger account a payout debits. It is never
  re-derived somewhere else, because two sources of truth for "how much money do I have"
  is how a merchant ends up seeing a figure they cannot actually withdraw.
</Callout>

## Getting paid

A merchant tells you one thing, and only when they want money out: **the bank account to
pay it into**.

You never ask for it at signup — a bank account is worth nothing until there is money to
send to it. When they first withdraw, they pick their bank from `GET /v1/banks` and enter
their account number; `POST /v1/payout-accounts/resolve` asks the bank who owns it, and
they confirm the bank's answer. The holder's name comes from the bank, never from a form
field, so a mistyped digit is caught before any money moves rather than after.

From then on, the balance sweeps to that account **once a day, automatically**. A merchant
who wants it sooner calls `POST /v1/settlements/payout` — which takes **no destination**,
because the money can only ever go to the account their bank already confirmed.

## Escrow and refunds

Settled funds are not instantly withdrawable. A recent-settlement **escrow hold** keeps
them for a window so a refund can be clawed back before the money leaves:

- **Refund**: reverses **only the organization's leg** back toward the payer; the platform
  fee is non-refundable and is never touched. A refund posts a new reversing transaction,
  so the ledger stays complete.
- **Payout**: withdraws the organization's available balance (settled net, minus the escrow
  hold and any minimum buffer).

<Callout type="note" title="Never faked, never stranded">
  A payout's ledger debit is committed **before** the bank transfer is attempted, so a crash
  mid-transfer can never roll back the record of money that is already moving. A transfer the
  bank *rejects* returns the funds to the merchant's balance; a transfer that is merely still
  *in flight* does not — pending is not failure, and treating it as one would pay the merchant
  twice.
</Callout>
